## 1. AI for Nonprofits: A project seeking to help Nonprofits benefit from generative AI tools and Agenforce.
The AI for Nonprofits team works to create helpful guides of prompts to make using AI tools like ChatGPT and Agentforce easier for Nonprofits. Led by Jana Walker and Katie Loescher.

Work performed at this sprint:
* AI Agent Development: Began building two AI "agents" in Salesforce‚Äîone for Donor Stewardship and another for event creation. They successfully added custom actions but encountered errors that need debugging.
* AI Tool Research & Content Creation: Researched and tested various free AI tools for nonprofit use cases and created a sample AI-generated video presentation using Lumen5.
* Documentation & Strategy: Brainstormed and documented OpenAI/Salesforce integration, including use cases, prompt best practices, risks, and cost analysis. They also began creating a "how-to" guide.
* Project Infrastructure: Set up key project management assets, including a dedicated Google Drive, a YouTube channel, and a Salesforce development environment (org and sandbox).

Next steps:
* Debug the AI Agent: Troubleshoot the errors preventing the agent from working correctly with the goal of successfully creating a campaign.
* Expand Documentation: Continue building out the "how-to" guides for the processes they are creating.
* Create Video Tutorials: Produce a step-by-step tutorial on how to create AI video content with Lumen5 and potentially record a demo of their Salesforce agent in action.

Join the [Commons Project: AI for Nonprofits](https://trailhead.salesforce.com/trailblazer-community/groups/0F9KX0000009xl90AA?tab=discussion&sort=LAST_MODIFIED_DATE_DESC) group in the Trailblazer Community to ask questions or get involved.

## 2. NEW: Chartiable Receipting: A project to help nonprofits master Canadian and US tax receipting for compliance and donor trust.
The new Receipting group brings together a team of volunteer experts to create clear, accessible resources that help Canadian and US nonprofits master charitable receipting.

Work performed at the Sprint:
* Created a comprehensive guide detailing the importance of receipting for legal compliance, audit readiness, and donor stewardship.
* Developed a side-by-side comparison of Canadian (CRA) and US (IRS) tax receipting requirements.
* Compiled a detailed matrix to evaluate and compare different receipting software solutions based on features, compliance, and data security.
* Clarified key compliance issues such as Canadian data residency, items that cannot be receipted, and the difference between a thank-you acknowledgment and an official tax receipt.

Next Steps:
* Create Promotional Content: Develop various materials to share the resource, including a Knowledge Article, a GitHub document, a presentation slide, and posts for LinkedIn and the Partner Community.
* Share with Key Communities: Post the guide and matrix to the Partner Community and the official "Sprinty Resources" page.
* Distribute to Influencers: Share the presentation slide with Nonprofit User Group Leaders to encourage wider adoption and awareness.

Next Steps:

## 5. Nonprofit Cloud Best Practices
Similar to early community-led efforts to help nonprofits find success using the NPSP, the goal of this project is to begin gathering ideas and feedback to see what the Nonprofit Cloud (NPC) best practices could look like and where the community could concentrate their efforts. Led by Dar Veverka, Tracey Braun, Junette Tan, Toby Ward, Judi Sohn, Shari Carlson.

The NPC Best Practices project is divided into several subgroups, each focusing on a key area. As a whole, the project successfully published new DPE (Data Protection and Encryption) guidance to the community repository. Below is a summary of each subgroup's progress.

Work performed at this sprint:

1) Program and Case Management

Accomplishments:
* Onboarded new team members to the sprint.
* Drafted two new use cases for program management.
* Streamlined their work by re-categorizing one use case to case management and removing another that was redundant.

Next Steps:
* Continue developing the remaining program management use cases.
* Begin working on case management use cases once the program-focused ones are complete.

2) ARC

Accomplishments:
* Brainstormed guidance documents and other resources to create for the community.
* Analyzed and discussed best practices for modeling complex households and other use cases.
* Created visual aids, including diagrams and screenshots, to support their recommendations.
* Set up a new Salesforce sandbox to facilitate their work.

Next Steps:
* Aim to complete the draft guidance document, including visuals and ARC "recipes," by the end of Friday.
* Identify potential automations that could be included in a future package.

3) Reporting

Accomplishments:
* Validated 35 reports and performed in-depth Quality Assurance (QA) on 17 of them.

Next Steps:
* Continue validating and performing QA on the remaining reports that are ready for testing.

Learn more about this project.

## 6. Summit Events App (SEA): Community-built event management solution for Nonprofits and Schools
Summit Events App is an end-to-end event management solution for everyone. SEA provides customizable event management, making registration, receipts, confirmation, and reporting easier and integrated with Salesforce. Led by Thaddaeus Dahlberg, Janeen Marquardt, Pieter Kop.

Work performed at the Sprint:
* Team Onboarding: Reviewed the existing application's functionality and successfully installed it for all team members.
* Strategic Planning: Analyzed two AI agents currently in development, defined their core "Jobs To Be Done" (JTBD), and established a clear plan for moving forward.

Next Steps:
* Resolve Blocker & Test: Address the current GitHub issue to allow for the installation of the testable app version and begin testing person accounts.
* Divide and Conquer AI Development: Split the AI team into two specialized groups:
* Group 1 (Technical): Will build the technical architecture for the "event creation" agent.
* Group 2 (Strategic): Will map the business process and define the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) for the "registration" agent to prepare it for development in the next sprint.

Join the Commons Project: Summit Events App group in the Trailblazer Community to ask questions or get involved. Learn more about this project!
